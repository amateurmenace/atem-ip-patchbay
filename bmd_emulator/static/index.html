Turn any video source into a remote input that streams into your ATEM Extreme G2, Television Studio, or Streaming Decoder over ethernet via SRT.
What it does

NDI, SDI, HDMI, non-Blackmagic SRT and RTMP streams can all be converted into the special Blackmagic flavor of SRT using this app, for free. This is more of a demo app showing what is now possible, a proof of concept, and should only be used in real productions at your own risk. It is free forever, until it either gets stopped by Blackmagic or they fully embrace opening up their stream decoding ecosystem.

How it happened

Made possible in part by the latest update to the iOS Blackmagic Camera App, which for the first time let that excellent free app stream directly into an input on a qualifying ATEM. Because it's software and not Blackmagic hardware, they seem to have lessened some of the handshake requirements between sender and receiver, so it was much easier to deconstruct what was essential and what was just noise. Then Claude and I (mostly Claude) built an emulator of a streaming encoder and eventually stumbled onto the winning combination that got a signal into the ATEM Mini Extreme ISO G2 that inspired this journey.

Made by Stephen Walter and Claude. Powered by Blackmagic Design.

                How do I find my switcher's format?
                

                  
ATEM Software Control: open the app →
                  Settings (gear icon, top-right) → Video →
                  Set video standard. Whatever's checked there is what
                  you pick here.

                  
From the unit itself: ATEM Mini Extreme G2,
                  Television Studio HD, and similar models display the current
                  standard on the front-panel screen — look for
                  1080p30 / 1080p59.94 / 1080p25 / etc.

                  
Common defaults:
                  1080p30 in NTSC regions (US, Canada, Japan),
                  1080p25 in PAL regions (most of Europe, Asia,
                  Australia), 1080p59.94 for broadcast-spec setups.

                  
If you set the wrong format here, the only
                  symptom is "stream connects but ATEM input shows black /
                  frozen / corrupt." Try cycling formats one by one until the
                  ATEM accepts.

            How does port forwarding work?
            

              
If your ATEM is at home and you're streaming from
              somewhere else (a venue, a coffee shop, another office),
              the stream has to traverse your home router. By default,
              routers block unsolicited inbound traffic
              — so you need to tell the router to forward SRT/RTMP
              packets to your ATEM.

              
    
                
  1. Open your router's admin page (commonly
                  192.168.1.1 or 192.168.0.1) —
                  credentials are usually printed on the bottom of the
                  unit.
    2. 
                
  2. Find Port Forwarding (sometimes
                  under "NAT", "Virtual Server", or "Advanced").
    3. 
                
  3. Add a rule: forward
                  UDP port 1935 for SRT (or
                  TCP port 1935 for RTMP) to your
                  ATEM's local IP (e.g. 192.168.1.50).
    4. 
                
  4. From the venue, point this app at your home's
                  public IP (look it up via
                  api.ipify.org),
                  not the ATEM's local IP.
    5. 
              

              
Some ISPs (mobile carriers, shared
              apartment-building connections) use
              carrier-grade NAT which silently blocks
              all inbound traffic regardless of your forwarding rules.
              Test by pushing from a phone on cellular data: if the
              stream reaches your ATEM, you're fine; if not, you'll
              need an SRT relay (a small VPS, Cloudflare TURN, or
              OvenMediaEngine) to bridge the gap.
